TAKE ACTION

There are many ways you can contribute to fighting climate change and supporting vulnerable island nations. Below are suggestions for how to get involved.

Learn More

Here is a list of credible resources to deepen your understanding of climate change impacts on Small Island Developing States (SIDS) and the global efforts for climate justice. Share this knowledge widely!


  • Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC) Reports. The most comprehensive and authoritative scientific assessments on climate change, its impacts, and future risks.
  • Pacific Islands Forum (PIF) Secretariat. Represents the political voice of Pacific Island countries, providing information on regional climate policies, advocacy efforts, and specific vulnerabilities.
  • United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change (UNFCCC) Website. The international treaty body that organizes global climate negotiations and provides official documentation on climate agreements and national commitments.

Advocate for Climate Justice

Developed nations and large corporations are major contributors to climate change, while small island nations like Fiji bear the brunt of its impacts. It's crucial to advocate for stronger emission reductions and financial support for adaptation and mitigation in vulnerable regions.


Your action item today is to contact policymakers in developed nations or representatives of international climate organizations. Urge them to commit to ambitious emission reduction targets and increase climate finance for Small Island Developing States.
